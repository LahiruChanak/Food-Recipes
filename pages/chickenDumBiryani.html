Chicken Dum Biryani

Difficulty

Beginner

Prep Time

12 hours

Cook Time

30 mins

Total Time

12 hours 30 mins

Servings

4

Description

The BEST chicken dum biryani recipe that actually gives you the perfect Biryani every time.

For the Marinade

For the Rice

For the Layering

Instructions

  1. You need about a cup of fried onions for this recipe. So if you don't already have fried onions, go ahead and deep fry about 2 cups of thinly sliced onions until crispy and golden.
  2. Make the marinade. Mix all the ingredients for the marinade except chicken and salt. Taste the mixture and add salt. Make sure to adjust the amount of salt I have given to your liking. You're going to add a lot of chicken to this. So make sure the marinade tastes a little too salty and sour.
  3. Now add the chicken pieces. Mix everything well together. I always use my hands for this. Then refrigerate for 12 hours to 24 hours. But if you're in a hurry, 3 to 4 hours would be just fine. I always marinate my chicken the day before I want to make biryani.
  4. Fill a large pot (4 liters) with water and bring to a boil. Add salt (it should taste like seawater). Add the remaining spices for the rice and the oil.
  5. Boil the rice until it's half done. You know it is done when you can crush it between two fingers, but it should still be too firm to eat.
  6. Layer your biryani. In a heavy bottomed, oven safe pan, put the ghee at the bottom. Layer the chicken and marinade at the bottom in an even layer. Add 1⁄2 of the chopped herbs and 1⁄3 of the fried onions. Then layer the rice on top. Then add the remaining herbs, fried onions and saffron milk.
  7. Now cover the pot. You can either use aluminum foil, or just put the lid on and make sure it's heavy and tight. In this picture, I have covered my pot with foil and then placed the lid on it.
  8. Heat a skillet/tawa on medium-high heat. Once it's hot, put the biryani pot on it and turn the heat to medium-low. Cook on dum/steam for about 30 minutes. This low and slow cooking helps everything cook evenly, without anything sticking to the bottom.
  9. Now open the pot. You will see that the rice is perfectly cooked and fluffy. The chicken has cooked through perfectly and is juicy and tender. Mix the biryani gently, fluffing the rice and serving chicken pieces on the side.

Enjoy your meal!
